    Jack Hermansson | Professional Career

    Jack started his professional mixed martial arts career in 2010. Having entered the fight to knock out opponents easily, Jack won his first professional fight in the East Coast Eight Factory organization.

    Subsequently, he also made two other victories in the British top promotion Cage Warriors. 

    Following his success, Jack signed with Bellator MMA in 2012 and made his promotional debut against Daniel Vizcaya on December 14, 2012.

    But there, Jack could not make many victories; he lost the back-and-forth fights by split decision.

    After that, Jack returned to Cage Warriors and started fighting there. Likewise, he became the Warrior Fight Series after defeating the UFC experienced fighter Karlos Vemola at Warrior Fight Series 4.

    With a successful career at Bellator MMA promotion, Cage Warriors, on May 31, 2016, Jack signed with the American biggest mixed martial arts promotion company (UFC) Ultimate Fight Championship. Jack mentioned in one of his interviews saying,

    “It means a great deal to me. To fight in the UFC is like a dream for me. It is one of my big goals in life. And I am so happy to be able to fight the best fighters in the world while the whole world is watching.”

    Likewise, he made his UFC debut against Scott Askham at UFC Fight Night 93 and won the match via unanimous decision on September 3, 2016.

    But in his next match against Cezar Ferreira on November 19, 2016, Jack lost through submission in the second round. 

    Nevertheless, the talented fighter dusted himself and went on a two-fight win streak against  Alex Nicholson and Brad Scott.

    Furthermore, going into the fight in May 2018, Jack broke his rib in the second round while facing Thales Leites.
