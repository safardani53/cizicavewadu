    So, you enjoy the delight of nail painting, but you despise spending just too much time cleaning the working area after each filing. This is where a nail dust collector comes in. While a nail dust collector may not have any direct impact on your ability to create a masterpiece on your customer's nails, it’s essential for maintaining a clean working environment that is safe for both you and your client. 

    After all, the best manicure is only part of what clients expect from a visit to your nail salon; you must always maintain a professional approach and provide them with an unforgettable experience. But, before you spend your money, you must know what makes a great quality nail dust collector. To help you out, we’ve put together this comprehensive buying guide — let’s take a look!

    How To Pick the Right Nail Dust Collector

    Power

    To live up to its name, a nail dust collector must be strong enough to snag the tiniest particles of dirt. Adequate ventilation should be your first consideration when maintaining a dust-free workspace. When the machine runs, its revolutions per minute (rpm) should be capable of swiftly swallowing dust and small particles.

    Design 

    The design of the dust collector includes both the way it looks and how it performs. Today, you can find quality nail dust collectors ranging in price from very inexpensive to very expensive, so you should easily be able to choose one that both meets your needs and doesn't break the bank.

    Dust bags

    Dust bags are useful for storing the nail dust generated by cutting and filing nails. Check the material dust bags are constructed of and whether or not they are reusable and washable.

    Noise

    Due to its huge motor, a nail dust collector can be extremely noisy. But a quality nail dust collector can operate efficiently with minimal noise, preserving a tranquil environment at the salon or home.

    Adjustable suction

    Not every manicure requires vigorous extraction, but an effective nail tool must be customizable. If the fan speed on the nail dust collector can be adjusted, that is unquestionably an advantage.

    Compact and portable 

    As a creative, you won’t want to deal with large, cumbersome tools; hence you should make sure your nail dust collector is small and lightweight.

    Easy operation and installation  

    Artists are meant to be making art, not reading lengthy user manuals. A simple, straightforward operation will help you save a great deal of time and effort.

    Reusable filter

    A filter should be replaced every three to four months; as such, reusable filters are far more practical, eco-friendly, and economical than disposable ones. 

    Benefits of Using Nail Dust Collectors

    Avoid respiratory illness and discomfort

    It is estimated that a cosmetician takes in 2 pounds of potentially harmful dust each year, 32% of which could lead to respiratory illness. If you use a nail dust collector, you won't have to worry about getting a stuffy nose or coughing up a lung because of all the dust.

    Less cleaning

    All dust generated during a manicure will be deposited directly into the nail dust collector. No longer will you have to clear off the annoying filth that accumulates in every corner of your station, which consequently affects your work productivity.

    Versatility 

    In general, a nail dust collector may accommodate all common uses for filing, shaping, and removing natural, gel, and artificial nails. Currently, there are numerous multifunctional options on the market, some of which combine a nail drill with an LED light.

    For the following situations, you may use a two-in-one or a three-in-one dust collector:

    • Your table isn't large enough for all these tools and you want to save space. 
    • You're also looking for a nail lamp or an electric file.
    • You want to get a good deal.
