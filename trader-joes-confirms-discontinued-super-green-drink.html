    Trader Joe's confirms three items you'll never see again - fans say they 'shed tears'

    02 Mar 2024, 00:00

    FANS of Trader Joe's are crying over a few items that they will never see again.

    The grocery chain's shoppers know products come and go, but that doesn't make it any easier when they have to say goodbye to one of their favorites.

    TikToker Kara Oley (@karaoley) has shared a video of some of the Trader Joe's products she still misses even though they've been gone for years.

    Also, Trader Joe's confirmed to The U.S. Sun that the following products have been discontinued.

    1. TABBOULEH-STYLE HUMMUS

    Customers loved this smooth and creamy spread that mimicked the taste of a tabbouleh salad.

    It featured chunks of tomato, parsley, onion, mint and a bit of lemon.

    read more on trader joe's

    Kara said she "literally shed a tear" when she found out the hummus was discontinued.

    2. THAI SWEET CHILI VEGGIE BURGERS

    These veggie patties were made out of a blend of Asian vegetables and rice.

    You could obviously serve them as a burger, but Kara said she used to chop them up and throw them into a stir fry.

    3. SUPER GREEN DRINK POWDER

    Trader Joe's offered this affordable option to help those who wanted to get a bit more veggies in their diet.

    Most read in Money

    Shoppers appreciated the berry flavor that masked the greens.

    On Trader Joe's Reviews, one shopper said they had used the product for 10 years before it was discontinued.

    Another person said it was their favorite green drink mix they had ever had.

    Nearly every reviewer asked for the mix to make a comeback, but for now, they'll have to find a replacement.

    A LIMITED-RELEASE PRODUCT

    Dill pickle mustard is one of Trader Joe's products it has under its limited release category.

    That said, fans can't get enough of it.

    It's made two appearances over the years but is currently listed as "not available" on Trader Joe's website.

    The recipe featured natural pickle flavoring, dill weed, and dicked up pickle pieces.

    The store recommends trying it not only on all the usual foods like hot dogs and burgers but also as a marinade, for devilled eggs or even potato salad for an added kick.

    THE GOOD NEWS

    Kara mentioned two other items she hasn't been able to find at her local Trader Joe's, but they're available at other stores.

    The first was the Truffle Aioli, which featured chunks of umami truffles throughout.

    While this is also a limited and price can carry by location, it's currently available on Trader Joe's website for $3.99.

    The other product is the store's vegan chocolate chip cookies.

    These aren't listed on the site, but Trader Joe's confirmed to The US Sun that they're available regionally.

    In her video, Kara said the cookies are only available on the west coast and asked the store to "please let the east coast girlies have some cookies too."
