    LIVERPOOL have been dealt a huge injury blow after Joe Gomez was forced to go off just ten minutes into England's clash with Holland.

    The 20-year-old fell awkwardly on his ankle after jumping on the ball, as physios rushed to the aid of the Reds defender.

    Gomez hobbled to the touchline, but was unable to shake off the problem, and was replaced by Leicester ace Harry Maguire.

    The former Charlton starlet was in visible pain as he made his way down the tunnel, as he was comforted by Three Lions boss Gareth Southgate.

    Jurgen Klopp will now be stressing that the injury isn't too serious ahead of the upcoming Champions League quarter-final tie against Manchester City.

    Gomez will almost certainly miss out of England's match against Italy at Wembley on Tuesday, with Burnley centre-back James Tarkowski vying to make his debut.

    Gomez will likely miss out on Liverpool's clash with Crystal Palace on March 31 at Selhurst Park.

    Following his impressive displays against Germany and Brazil back in December, the youngster was looking to cement his place in the side ahead of the upcoming World Cup.
