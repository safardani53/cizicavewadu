    A CREATIVE TikToker demonstrated how to make an expensive popular pottery barn Halloween pillow for just $15-worth of material.

    The decor piece, known as “Gus the Ghost” is an adorable stuffed ghost pillow – but it originally cost $80 and is "no longer available" on the Pottery Barn website.

    Thankfully, one crafty social media user @baerart13 made the perfect replica of Gus, easily and cheaply.

    She took a white blanket and draped it over a stuffed animal in a cardboard box, before putting felt oval eyes on the blanket.

    "I decided to make my own Gus the Ghost from Pottery Barn and instead of paying $80 I made him for $15,” she said.

    The pillow looked identical to the Pottery barn version 

    “Crafted of spherical pillows with a cozy sherpa overlay, Gus makes a snuggly and whimsical addition to your sofa or children’s bedroom,” Pottery Barn’s website read.

    The U.S. Sun has previously reported on another excellent dupe by Design expert Kristyn Cole, who recreated Pottery Barn's Skeleton Candy Bowl.

    The bowl has a skeleton figure attached with a silver finish.

    Pottery Barn charges $199 - but Kristyn can remake the bowl for $30.

    Most read in The US Sun

    She gets a large bowl from Target for $9 and a skeleton figure from Amazon for around $15.

    Kristyn placed the bowl on top of the skeleton, before extending its arms out to hold the top of the bowl.

    "The bowl fits perfectly right under his ribcage," she explained, before glueing the skeleton to the bowl.

    "I secured it in any part his body was actually touching to make it more durable," Kristyn went on.

    Kristyn uses the $12.98 Krylon Looking Glass Silver Spray Paint from Amazon to make it look titanium.

    For an antique-like finish, she adds streaks of black acrylic paint to the outside of the bowl.

    "Here's the finished look," she told her followers. "You can literally put him on a shelf and his legs can dangle down.

    "Or you push him back and have him sit completely flat."

    Viewers couldn't believe how much better they thought Kristyn's bowl looked compared to the Pottery Barn one.
