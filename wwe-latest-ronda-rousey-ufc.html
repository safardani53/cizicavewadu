    RONDA ROUSEY is remaining tight-lipped on her future after she was used in her first WWE storyline.

    The former UFC champ offered to face wrestling superstar trio Charlotte Flair, Becky Lynch and Bayley "anytime, anywhere" in a recent taping.

    And that had fight fans around the globe frantic with excitement.

    Rousey was spotted arriving at the final of the Mae Young Classic in Las Vegas this week and was asked about a potential switch to WWE, where she stated she was just there to support Shayna.

    She said: "I don't want to be the kind of person that comes to support her friend and then talks about herself all night. It's all about her."

    Shayna Bazsler was Rousey's old MMA training partner and reached the final two of the wrestling tournament.

    She was also supported by Rowdy's fellow partners Jessamyn Duke and Marina Shafir, which led to speculation the "The Four Horsewomen" of UFC would make the move from octagon to the wrestling ring.

    Rousey has already trained at WWE's performance centre in Florida and joined The Rock in confronting Triple H and Stephanie McMahon at WrestleMania.

    The 30-year-old has made no secret of her happiness at seeing the increased platform given the female wrestlers over recent years.

    She said: "It's been great to watch the role of women really evolve over the past few years, it's really encouraging.

    "A lot of progress has been made but, as a lot of industries, I'm excited to be here to watch it happen."
