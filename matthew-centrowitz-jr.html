    Besides that, the athlete runs specifically in the 1500m event. Likewise, the runner competed in the Tokyo 2020 Olympics to defend his Olympic gold at the 1500m event.

    Matthew Centrowitz Jr. | Early Life, Family, And Education

    Matthew Centrowitz Jr. was born in Beltsville, Maryland, the United States. His parents are Matt Centrowitz and Beverly Bannister Centrowitz.

    The athlete’s father is also a renowned runner and two-time Olympian. Matt competed in the 1500m race at the 1976 Summer Olympics. 

    Moreover, he was also selected to participate in the 1980 Summer Olympics but did not compete due to the 1980 Summer Olympics boycott.

    Besides that, Matt Sr. is a long-distance runner who has won four gold medals at the USA Outdoor Championships in the 5000m race. Likewise, he also won a gold medal at the 1979 Pan American Games held in San Juan, Puerto Rico. 

    After his career as an athlete, he became a track coach and worked at St. John’s University and American University. Furthermore, Matt was the Cross Country and Track and Field Director at Manhattan College. 

    However, he recently stepped down from that position and was succeeded by his colleague Kerri Gallagher.

    Similarly, Matthew’s mother, Beverly, is also a track and field athlete who competed for four years with Hunter College and Cross-country teams. 

    She was quite successful in her career, won 10 CUNYAC titles, and was a two-time New York State ½ mile champion. Not to mention, Beverly was Hunter’s first national class women’s runner. 

    Hence, it is safe for us to say that running is in Matthew Centrowitz’s blood. He was encouraged and coached by his parents from a young age.

    Education

    The gold medalist attended high school at Broadneck High School. He ran track there and was well-known for his speed. 

    After his high school graduation, Matthew attended the same college his father went to, i.e., the University of Oregon. He ran for the Oregon Ducks and had a pretty successful collegiate career.

    Matthew Centrowitz Jr. | Running and Olympics Career

    High School and Collegiate Career

    At Broadneck High School, he ran at the Nike Outdoor Nationals, where he completed the second half of the race faster than the first half and won the competition.

    Moreover, his technique was well praised, and experts considered it one of the best races in prep history. Likewise, at Penn Relays, the three-time Olympian set a meet record. 

    Furthermore, as a high schooler, he won the Pan American Junior Championships. Thereafter, he ran for the Oregon Ducks, where he broke the NCAA 4 x mile record with his teammates. 

    Similarly, he was victorious at the NCAA Men’s Division I Outdoor Track and Field Championships’ 1500m race. Not to mention, he won the Pac-10’s 1500 four times by 2011. 

    Professional Career

    Early Career and First Olympics

    Finally, he decided to turn pro at the end of November 2011 and became a Nike athlete shortly after. Moreover, Matthew participated in the Nike Oregon Project, where Alberto Salazar trained him.

    His first season as a pro athlete was one of his best seasons. In 2011, Centrowitz won NCAA Championship, the national championship, and earned a bronze medal at the IAAF World Championships in Athletics. 

    Besides that, the runner qualified for the 2012 London Olympics. However, he failed to secure a bronze medal by .04 seconds and finished in fourth place.  

    Furthermore, his 2013 season was a bit better than in 2012. He won the 800m race at the Seattle UW Indoor Preview, finished second at the Millrose Games Wanamaker Mile, and first at the New Balance Indoor Grand Prix mile.

    Likewise, the athlete won the 2013 USATF Outdoor 1500m championship and got selected to run at the IAAF World Championships. Again, he made it to the finals, where he was awarded a silver medal.

    First Olympic Gold Medal and Qualifying for the Tokyo 2020 Olympics

    In 2016, he had an outstanding indoor season where he won gold at the World Indoor Championships. Shortly after that, he qualified for his second consecutive Olympic appearance.

    Recently, in 2021, the Oregon runner qualified for the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. He is well determined to win another gold in the 1500m race in his third Olympic appearance.
